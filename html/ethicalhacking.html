About Ethical Hacking

Ethical hacking involves an authorized attempt to gain unauthorized access to a computer system, application, or data.

What is Ethical Hacking?

Ethical hacking, also known as penetration testing or white-hat hacking, involves the same tools, tricks, and techniques that hackers use, but with one major difference: Ethical hacking is legal.

Types of Ethical Hackers

Ethical hackers are usually categorized as white-hat hackers (who exploit security systems on behalf of their owners, and who are considered 'good hackers').

Why Ethical Hacking?

Ethical hacking is performed to identify potential threats on a computer or network that can be exploited by malicious hackers.

Ethical Hacking Techniques

Some common techniques include: vulnerability scanning, password cracking, virus creation, packet sniffing, and rootkit creation.

Ethical Hacking in the Enterprise

Many businesses employ ethical hackers to help improve their systems and networks by identifying and fixing potential vulnerabilities.
